STEP-BY-STEP APPROACH (follow this reasoning process):
1. First, identify all section headings and the overall structure of the NDA
2. Group all related content under each section heading into a single clause
3. Assign the most specific clause_type from the list below that matches each clause's content

GUIDELINES:
1. Identify major section headings and group all related content under a single clause
2. Do NOT split sections with the same heading (e.g., all "Confidentiality Obligations" content should be in one clause)
3. Each clause should represent a complete legal concept
4. Preserve the original wording and formatting exactly
5. Include clause numbers or headings if present in the original
6. Ensure no important content is lost or duplicated

CLAUSE TYPES:
Use the most specific applicable type from this list:

Core NDA Clauses:
- definitions — Defined terms, interpretation rules
- confidentiality — Non-disclosure obligations, information protection
- permitted_disclosures — Exclusions from confidentiality (public info, prior knowledge, compelled disclosure)
- obligations — Receiving/disclosing party duties, standard of care
- term_and_duration — Agreement duration, effective date, renewal
- termination — Termination rights, events, post-termination duties
- return_of_materials — Return or destruction of confidential information

Enforcement Clauses:
- remedies — Injunctive relief, damages, breach consequences
- indemnification — Hold harmless, defense obligations (if present)
- non_compete — Competition restrictions (if bundled with NDA)
- non_solicitation — Employee/client solicitation restrictions (if bundled)

Standard Legal Clauses:
- governing_law — Choice of law, jurisdiction
- dispute_resolution — Arbitration, mediation, litigation
- notices — Communication requirements, delivery methods
- assignment — Transfer of rights
- amendments — Modification procedures
- severability — Survival of valid provisions
- entire_agreement — Integration clause
- waiver — Waiver of rights provisions
- recitals — Whereas/background clauses
- execution — Signature blocks, counterparts
- miscellaneous — Anything that does not fit any category above (last resort only)

- Be thorough and accurate — every sentence in the document must appear in exactly one clause
- Maintain legal precision in preserving original text
- Only use "miscellaneous" as a last resort when no other type fits
- If a clause clearly spans multiple types, choose the dominant/primary type
